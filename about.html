Who I am

Bridging thoughtful research with human-centered AI products

I'm a Data Science graduate student at Indiana University Bloomington with a focus on building applied machine learning systems that make complex information accessible. From large language model research to explainable reinforcement learning, I enjoy exploring the intersection of rigorous experimentation and human impact.

Bloomington, IN Machine Learning & AI Open-source contributor

Motivation

In 2018, during my sophomore year, I watched DeepMind's film on AlphaGo. The 37th move in that legendary match with Lee Sedol changed how I saw the future: it was a human–machine duet rather than a competition. Since then I have pursued projects that strengthen the symbiotic relationship between people and intelligent systems, from open-source contributions to production-grade agents.

Interests & downtime

Outside of research and product building, I write about abstract ideas, conspiracy theories, the future of AI, and economic shifts. You can find my essays and musings on my blog. I also play the guitar—John Mayer's phrasing and tone never fail to inspire me.

At a glance
